so Cranberry Juice is basically my own music player that i've been building for elementary OS, and its a real native app, not one of those electron web-wrapper things! it just reads a folder of your music off your computer and plays it locally, no accounts, no cloud, nothing phoning home. i wanted something that feels like a proper desktop player but actually mine.

you point it at your music folder and it scans everything, caches all the cover art so its quick, and you can browse by song, album, artist, genre, all that. theres a mini player that shrinks down to just the album cover, shuffle with a real Up Next queue you can reorder, and it hooks into the sound indicator so you can control it from up top.

the fun part honestly is the audio-reactive visuals, a whole gallery of fullscreen visualizers that move with the music. and theres a bunch of themes - dark glass, light, cranberry red, leaf green, deep blue, just peachy (salmon pink and glassy with little gold stars on your clicks), and grimace which is dark purple with iridescent accents.

oh and theres optional Discogs support for artist photos and better genre tags if you add a token, otherwise it stays totally offline. thats it really, just a clean local music player i made!
